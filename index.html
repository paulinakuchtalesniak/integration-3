Important dates

1987

The French government decides to build the high-speed line from Paris to the Channel Tunnel/Belgian border.

1989

The ministers from the four countries reach an agreement on the high-speed network in The Hague.

1990

Belgium approves the project. At this point, the construction of the Channel Tunnel, a key part of the network connecting to London, is already underway.

2009

The PBKAL high-speed rail network, linking France, Belgium, Germany, and the Netherlands, was completed in 2009. This project, starting in the mid-1980s, established acrucial transportation link across Europe.

new face of the railways

To comply with European regulations, NMBS/SNCB, established in 1926, divided into three entities on January 1, 2005: NMBS/SNCB , Infrabel and HR-Rail ,collectively forming the SNCB-Group.

NMBS/SNCB

↓

NMBS/SNCB operates the passenger trains and maintains the rolling stock. It also owns all station buildings and manages all the stations and stops.

INFRABEL

↓

Infrabel manages and modernises the Belgian railway infrastructure and controls the train traffic. Safety comes first in this. The company is also responsible for the allocation of train paths to all railway operators.

HR RAIL

↓

HR-Rail, the legal employer of the entire complement of railway personnel, was founded. Its responsibility lies with offering an integrated HR service to NMBS/SNCB and Infrabel, including social consultation and recruitment.

Modernisations of HLS in Belgium

Investment & modernisation

2011

NMBS/SNCB has launched 305 'Desiro Main Line' electric units, designed for local and regional travel. These lightweight trains, featuring a low-floor section, are perfectly suited for routes with multiple stops, enhancing passenger convenience.

2012

The Diabolo, the northern access to Zaventem (Brussels Airport), was ready in 2012. Part of the Diabolo is a new railway line between Mechelen and Schaerbeek, largely in the middle verge of the E19 motorway. This line is linked to the airport via a railway triangle.

In 2020, NMBS/SNCB launched the first of 445 new M7 double-decker carriages, adding 50,000 seats to the network.
