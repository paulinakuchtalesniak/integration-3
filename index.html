History of High Speed Lines Development in Belgium

Hsl idea

At the 1986 annual conference of the Ministers of Transport for France, Germany, Belgium and The Netherlands, a high-speed network between Paris – Brussels - Cologne/Amsterdam, or PBKAL for short (the first letters of each connected city) was discussed for the first time. The L referred to London, which would also be connected to this network via the Channel Tunnel.

Important dates

1987

The French government decides to build the high-speed line from Paris to the Channel Tunnel/Belgian border.

1989

The ministers from the four countries reach an agreement on the high-speed network in The Hague.

1990

Belgium approves the project. At this point, the construction of the Channel Tunnel, a key part of the network connecting to London, is already underway.

2009

The PBKAL high-speed rail network, linking France, Belgium, Germany, and the Netherlands, was completed in 2009. This project, starting in the mid-1980s, established acrucial transportation link across Europe.
